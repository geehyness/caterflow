Caterflow User Guide

Your complete guide to managing inventory with Caterflow

Getting Started with Caterflow

1

Logging In

Access Caterflow using your credentials. Your system administrator will provide your login details.

If you can't log in, contact your system administrator to ensure your account is properly set up.
2

Understanding the Interface

The Caterflow interface consists of three main areas:

  • Dashboard: Overview of current stock levels and alerts.
  • Modules: Access to specific functions like Inventory, Purchasing, and Reporting.
  • Settings: Configuration options for your account and site.

Key Features

Real-time Stock Tracking

Monitor your inventory levels instantly, get alerts for low stock, and generate reports on stock movement.

Purchase Order Management

Create and track purchase orders from suppliers, and manage goods receipt to update inventory automatically.

Master Data Management

1

Stock Items

Define and manage all your inventory items. Each item has a unique SKU, name, unit of measure, and category.

2

Sites and Bins

Organize your physical locations. A Site represents a building or major location, while Bins are specific storage areas within that site.

Inventory Operations

1

Receiving Items

When a new delivery arrives, use the Goods Receipt module to log the items, quantities, and their storage bin.

2

Dispatching Items

When items are removed from inventory, create a Dispatch Log to track their destination and purpose.

Actions & Workflows

1

Internal Transfers

Use this function to move items between bins or sites. The system will automatically update stock levels in both locations.

2

Stock Adjustments

Make manual adjustments for damaged, expired, or lost items to maintain accurate records.

Reporting & Analytics

The reporting module provides valuable insights into your inventory performance.

  • Stock Reports: View current stock levels, low-stock items, and valuation.
  • Transaction Reports: Track all inventory movements, including receipts, dispatches, and adjustments.
  • Audit Trail: Every change is logged with a user and timestamp for accountability.

System Administration

Administrators can manage users and system-wide settings.

  • User Management: Add, edit, and deactivate user accounts.
  • Permissions: Assign roles to control access to different modules.
