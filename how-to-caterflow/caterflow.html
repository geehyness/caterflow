Caterflow User Guide

Your complete guide to managing inventory with Caterflow

About Caterflow

Caterflow is a comprehensive inventory management system designed specifically for catering operations, healthcare facilities, and other organizations requiring precise stock control. The system helps you:

  • Track inventory levels in real-time across multiple locations
  • Manage purchase orders and supplier relationships
  • Streamline receiving, dispatching, and internal transfers
  • Maintain accurate records for auditing and compliance
  • Generate insightful reports for better decision-making

The system uses a sophisticated calculation method that combines physical counts with transaction data to provide accurate, real-time stock levels.

Getting Started with Caterflow

1

Logging In

Access Caterflow using your credentials. Your system administrator will provide your login details.

If you can't log in, contact your system administrator to ensure your account is properly set up.
2

Understanding the Interface

The Caterflow interface consists of three main areas:

  • Dashboard: Overview of current stock levels and alerts.
  • Modules: Access to specific functions like Inventory, Purchasing, and Reporting.
  • Settings: Configuration options for your account and site.

Key Features

Real-time Stock Tracking

Monitor your inventory levels instantly, get alerts for low stock, and generate reports on stock movement.

Purchase Order Management

Create and track purchase orders from suppliers, and manage goods receipt to update inventory automatically.

Best Practices for Using Caterflow

Regular Physical Counts

Conduct regular inventory counts to maintain data accuracy. The system calculates current stock based on the latest count plus subsequent transactions.

Recommendation: Schedule counts based on item criticality - high-value items weekly, others monthly.

Timely Transaction Recording

Record all inventory movements (receiving, dispatching, transfers) immediately after they occur to maintain real-time accuracy.

Recommendation: Designate specific times for transaction recording and make it part of your standard operating procedures.

Consistent Naming Conventions

Use consistent naming for items, bins, and sites to make searching and reporting more effective.

Recommendation: Develop a naming convention document and ensure all staff are trained on it.

Supplier Management

Keep supplier information up-to-date and assign primary suppliers to items for more efficient ordering.

Recommendation: Review supplier performance quarterly and update information as needed.

Proper User Role Assignment

Assign appropriate roles and permissions to users based on their responsibilities to maintain security and data integrity.

Recommendation: Regularly review user access levels, especially when staff roles change.

Utilize the Audit Trail

Leverage the system's audit trail functionality to track changes, investigate discrepancies, and maintain accountability.

Recommendation: Review audit logs weekly for unusual activities or patterns.

Regular System Training

Ensure all users receive proper training on the system and stay updated on new features or changes.

Recommendation: Schedule refresher training sessions quarterly and when new features are released.

Master Data Management

1

Stock Items

Define and manage all your inventory items. Each item has a unique SKU, name, unit of measure, and category.

2

Sites and Bins

Organize your physical locations. A Site represents a building or major location, while Bins are specific storage areas within that site.

Inventory Operations

1

Receiving Items

When a new delivery arrives, use the Goods Receipt module to log the items, quantities, and their storage bin.

2

Dispatching Items

When items are removed from inventory, create a Dispatch Log to track their destination and purpose.

Actions & Workflows

1

Internal Transfers

Use this function to move items between bins or sites. The system will automatically update stock levels in both locations.

2

Stock Adjustments

Make manual adjustments for damaged, expired, or lost items to maintain accurate records.

Reporting & Analytics

The reporting module provides valuable insights into your inventory performance.

  • Stock Reports: View current stock levels, low-stock items, and valuation.
  • Transaction Reports: Track all inventory movements, including receipts, dispatches, and adjustments.
  • Audit Trail: Every change is logged with a user and timestamp for accountability.

System Administration

Administrators can manage users and system-wide settings.

  • User Management: Add, edit, and deactivate user accounts.
  • Permissions: Assign roles to control access to different modules.
